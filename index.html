The sunken temple - Kanchanaburi, Thailand

I have received Consolation Prize in "My Hometown" Photography Exhibit (VIRTUAL EXHIBIT: SEPTEMBER - OCTOBER, 2020).
The sunken temple was built in 1950 and sunk in 1968 after the construction of Khao Laem Dam. The only structure from the submerged town that remains today is the Sunken Temple. Today, during the rainy season when the reservoir levels are high, the top half and the steeple are the only parts of the temple that are visible, and a boat ride takes you around the curious structure. When the region is drier, it is possible to walk around and inside to take a closer look at the ruins.

The My Hometown exhibit is a juried photography contest open to international students studying at Texas Tech University.
